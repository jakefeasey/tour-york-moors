With its impressive sea cliffs to the east and huge views of the distant Pennines to the west, the North York Moors is one of the best places in the UK for a cycling adventure. Over the past couple of years we have been spending more and more time exploring this harsh but beautiful place. During the course of these trips the idea came to put together a route that other could use to explore some of its best spots.

Made up of three approximately 100km sections (West, North, and East), the route is mostly bridleways/single track with as little tarmac as possible. The route takes you through Dalby Forest & Sutton Bank trails as well as some known riding spots like Guisborough Woods & the Whitby/Scarborough Cinder Track. Along the way is an abundance of brilliant local cafes, B&Bs, and camping spots.

A Word of Warning

Although beautiful the North York Moors are a brutal landscape, renowned for their changeable weather and lack of shelter. It can be easy to get caught out up there if you are not prepared.

Even if the weather is looking on your side, make sure you bring what you need should it turn on you or if you run into trouble. The terrain presents a challenge in itself also, there will be sections of the route that are not ridable so be prepared to hike-a-bike on occasion.
