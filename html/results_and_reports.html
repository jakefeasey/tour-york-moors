Summer 2020

Why and How?

Over the past few years I've been spending progressively more time exploring the North York Moors. Every ride out there has shown me further how much great riding there is to be done out there, as well as some particular bridleways/tracks that are worth avoiding.
When the COVID-19 lockdown came into effect and all the events I had lined up for 2020 got cancelled I found myself with more time and a need for some kind of project outside of work. I decided that I would put together a route that would take a few days of riding and would explore as much of the moors as possible on as little paved roads as I could. While out riding one day I decided I'd use my technical know how to create a website so I could share this route and the resources I've found along the way with others.
The route will definitely change occasionally, so keep an eye on here or on the Instagram account I’ve created for this. Changes will be either if I find something on the route that doesn’t work or find a better way around something. I’ll try to validate any changes I make in a short post somewhere on this website.
Since I had this idea, I’ve been trying to gather info on local resources and drawing all over my OS maps. Now I’ve got a version 1 of the route and a half decent run down of helpful points of interest. I’m always interested in suggestions or improvements, so if you have any get in touch!
The route was initially just for fun, but I’m also interested in ITT style bikepacking races so I’m going to try and keep track of fastest times round the route. Of course, this will rely on honesty of riders and for those riders to let me know what their times are. If you’re going to ITT it then please remember to be kind to everyone else you find on the trails, everyone’s enjoyment of the outside is more important that your time around the route. I’d also ask that, if you’re ITT’ing it, you don’t do things like cache gear, or pre-arrange things, basically follow the usual ITT rules of (much) bigger events like The Tour Divide. At some point I’ll probably write out some ITT rules and put them in their own special place on the website.
